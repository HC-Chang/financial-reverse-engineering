Stress Test: Single Scenario vs. Portfolio Score

How should we evaluate your plan against historical "disasters"?

A

Single "Worst Case" Focus

Picks one extreme historical event (e.g., 2008) and shows exactly what happens to your balance. Gives you one specific "Extra Fuel" number to survive that one event.

2008 Stress Test

⚠️ Plan Failed at Year 12

Requires +$450/mo extra fuel to survive.

B

Multi-Scenario "Risk Score"

Runs your plan against 5-10 historical periods (1929, 1973, 2000, 2008, etc.) and gives you a combined "Resilience Score." Tells you which specific eras are your biggest threats.

Resilience Score: 72/100

✅ Survived 7 of 10 historical crashes.

❌ Struggled with high-inflation (1970s).

Why it matters

The **Single Scenario** is more tangible and easier to act on, while the **Risk Score** provides a broader view of different types of threats (market crashes vs. inflation spikes).
