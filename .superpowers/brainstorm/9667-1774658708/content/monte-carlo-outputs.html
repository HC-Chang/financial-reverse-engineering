Monte Carlo: What's the primary "Answer"?

Monte Carlo simulations run thousands of scenarios. How should we summarize them for you?

A

Success Probability

A single "Confidence Score" (e.g., 85%). Simple, actionable, and tells you if you're on track or need to adjust.

85%
B

Outcome Distribution

A "Fan Chart" showing the range of possibilities. Shows the "median" path vs. the lucky/unlucky extremes.

C

Safety Buffer / Stress Test

Focuses on the "failure" cases. Tells you the specific "Extra Fuel" needed to survive a historical 1929 or 2008 event.

Why it matters

The **Success Rate** is best for confidence, the **Distribution** is best for visualizing uncertainty, and the **Stress Test** is best for aggressive "disaster-proofing" your plan.
